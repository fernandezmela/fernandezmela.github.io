About Me

I’m Melanie Fernández, a computer science graduate interested in machine learning, research engineering, and applied artificial intelligence. My work has focused on neural networks for strategic decision-making, including Shogi match prediction and meta-learning approaches for Chess and Shogi position evaluation.

I’m especially interested in building systems that connect research ideas to usable tools: clean pipelines, interpretable experiments, reproducible code, and applications that make technical work easier to understand.
