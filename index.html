About Me

I'm Melanie Fernández, a computer science graduate with a focus on machine learning, data science, and applied AI. My research background is in meta-learning and neural networks for strategic decision-making — including a deep learning model for Shogi match prediction and a meta-learning (MAML) framework for cross-game position evaluation in Chess and Shogi, trained on tens of thousands of games.

Alongside research, I've worked as a data analyst and teaching assistant, building SQL- and Python-based analysis pipelines and helping students work through algorithms and computational intelligence coursework. I'm drawn to problems where rigorous modeling meets real data: building pipelines that are reproducible, interpretable, and grounded in the question they're meant to answer.

I'm currently looking for roles in data science, applied AI, and machine learning research and engineering, and exploring graduate programs in machine learning and AI.
